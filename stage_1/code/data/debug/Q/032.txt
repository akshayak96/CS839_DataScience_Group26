<person>Bekele</> sets sights on world mark

Olympic 10,000m champion <person>Kenenisa Bekele</> is determined to add the world indoor two mile record at February's Norwich Union Grand Prix in Birmingham.

The 22-year-old will again be chasing a record held by his compatriot and mentor <person>Haile Gebrselassie</>, who set the mark at the same meeting in 2003. "I am still as hungry to do as much as I can in this sport," said <person>Bekele</>. "And aiming for the two mile world record in Birmingham is the next of those targets." <person>Gebrselassie</>'s current record stands at eight minutes, 04.69 seconds. And <person>Bekele</> is no stranger to overhauling world marks at the National Indoor Arena. The Ethiopian broke the world indoor 5,000m record on his debut at the meeting last year. Compatriots <person>Mulugeta Wondimu</>, <person>Abiyote Abate</> and <person>Markos Geneti</>, the world indoor bronze medallist over 3000m, will race against <person>Bekele</> on 18 February. The meet has already attracted a crop of Olympic talent. Britain's 800m and 1500m champion <person>Kelly Holmes</> is taking part in the 1000m. Swedish heptathlon gold medallist <person>Carolina Kluft</> will contest the 60m hurdles. While men's 4x100m relay gold medallists <person>Jason Gardener</> and <person>Mark Lewis-Francis</> will go head-to-head in the 60m.
